Threading Together a Story

A torn hem. A rainy afternoon. And too much tea. It started there. I wasn’t planning to create anything new — I was fixing something old. But then I saw it: the rip looked like a line on a map, and I started stitching around it. The repair became a border, the border became a frame, and suddenly it wasn’t a fix — it was the whole point.

This is why I always say: don’t throw away the torn thing. It might just be the seed of your next idea.

Stitching in Silence

Sometimes when I teach workshops, people get quiet. I used to worry it meant they were bored — but I’ve learned to recognize that kind of silence: it’s focus. It’s presence.

There’s a rhythm to stitching. Hands move, thoughts settle. And in that stillness, something often unspoken is shared — a sense of making time tangible.

The Beauty of Mending

Mending has become more than repair for me — it’s a practice of seeing worth in what’s worn. Every patch is a decision to keep loving something a little longer.

In a world that asks for constant newness, choosing to mend is quietly radical. And beautiful.
